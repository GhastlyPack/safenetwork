How Card Grading Works: PSA vs CGC vs SGC

MaxoCollects Sports Cards Host at Safe Network ¬∑ @maxo_collects
üíé

The difference between a PSA 9 and a PSA 10 of the same card can be thousands of dollars. I have seen it on our shows over and over - a collector submits a card expecting a 10, gets back a 9, and the financial impact is immediate and real. Understanding how grading works is not optional knowledge for serious collectors. It is the difference between building a collection that holds value and one that consistently underperforms. Here is everything you need to know.

What Is Card Grading?

Card grading is the process of sending your trading card to a professional third-party authentication and grading company. Trained graders examine the card under magnification, evaluate it across four core categories, assign a numeric grade on a 1 to 10 scale, and then seal the card in a tamper-evident plastic case called a slab. That slab carries the grade on its label permanently.

The purpose of grading is twofold. First, it authenticates the card - confirming it is genuine and has not been altered, trimmed, or restored. Second, it establishes a universally recognized condition standard. A PSA 10 means the same thing to a buyer in Tokyo as it does to one in Texas. That standardization creates a liquid, trustworthy marketplace for high-value cards.

What Graders Actually Evaluate

Every major grading company evaluates cards across four categories: centering (how well-centered the image is within the borders), corners (sharpness and wear at all four corners), edges (condition of the card's perimeter), and surface (print quality, scratches, stains, and gloss). A card must score well across all four to achieve a top grade - one weak category can drop an otherwise flawless card from a 10 to an 8.

The Grading Scale Explained

All three major grading companies use the same 1 to 10 numeric scale, though their standards for each grade differ slightly. Here is what each tier means in practical terms:

10
Gem MintPerfect or near-perfect in every category. Four sharp corners, flawless centering, no surface wear visible to the naked eye. The standard every submitter hopes for.
9
MintOne minor flaw - a slightly off-center image, a barely detectable corner touch, or a minor print defect. Still an exceptional card but noticeably less valuable than a 10.
8
Near Mint to MintLight wear on one or two corners, minor centering issues, or a small surface mark. Still a sharp, desirable card for most collectors.
7

PSA vs CGC vs SGC

The three major grading companies each have distinct strengths, market positioning, and collector communities. Choosing the right one for your cards matters - both for turnaround time and the premium your graded card will command on the secondary market.

PSA

The most recognized brand in sports card grading. PSA grades carry the highest market premiums for most sports cards and are the default choice for high-value modern cards.

HIGHEST PREMIUMS
CGC

The preferred grader for Pokemon, Magic: The Gathering, and non-sport cards. CGC's card division has grown rapidly and is gaining ground in sports as well.

BEST FOR POKEMON
SGC

Known for strong vintage sports card grading and faster turnaround times. SGC is gaining collector respect and offers competitive pricing for many submission tiers.

Why Grading Impacts Value

The financial impact of a single grade point is one of the most counterintuitive things about the card market until you see it in action. Here is a real-world example of how dramatic the spread can be:

  • A 2003 LeBron James Topps Chrome rookie in PSA 8 might sell for $800 to $1,200
  • The same card in PSA 9 could bring $3,000 to $5,000
  • In PSA 10, that card has sold for $30,000 to $100,000+

That is not an anomaly. It is the structure of the graded card market. PSA publishes population reports showing how many copies of each card exist at each grade - and when PSA 10 population is low for a key card, the scarcity premium is real and significant.

"Grading transforms a card from a subjective conversation about condition into an objective, verifiable fact. That objectivity is what makes the high-value card market function. Without it, every transaction would be a negotiation about condition rather than a negotiation about price."

Should You Get Your Cards Graded?

The general rule is this: if a card is worth $150 or more in top raw condition and you believe it has a realistic shot at a PSA 9 or 10, grading makes financial sense. Below that threshold, grading fees eat into or eliminate any value gained. For cards you plan to hold long term regardless of financial return, grading provides authentication and preservation value that matters independently of price.

Frequently Asked Questions

Turnaround times vary by service level and grading company. PSA economy service can take 6 to 12 months during busy periods. Express and premium tiers at PSA, CGC, and SGC can return cards in 2 to 8 weeks. Faster tiers cost significantly more per card.

PSA carries the highest market premiums for most sports cards and is the most widely recognized brand. CGC is preferred for Pokemon and non-sport cards. SGC is gaining traction for vintage sports cards and offers faster turnaround at competitive prices. The best company depends on the type of card and where you plan to sell.

A PSA 10 Gem Mint is the highest grade PSA awards and represents a card with four perfectly sharp corners, no visible surface wear, flawless centering, and no print defects visible to the naked eye. PSA 10s routinely sell for 3x to 10x the price of the same card in PSA 9, making the difference between grades extremely significant financially.
